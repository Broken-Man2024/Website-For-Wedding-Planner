A Muslim wedding, also known as a Nikah or Islamic marriage ceremony, is a sacred and joyous occasion that follows Islamic traditions and customs. Here's a brief summary of the key elements typically involved in a Muslim wedding:.

Proposal and Arrangement:

The process often begins with a proposal, where the groom or his family formally approaches the bride's family to seek her hand in marriage.

Once both families agree, a formal arrangement is made, and the couple may have the opportunity to meet and consent to the marriage.

Nikkah

Before the wedding, the groom provides the bride with a gift known as the Mahr, which is a mandatory part of the marriage contract. It symbolizes the financial security and commitment of the husband to the wife.

Walima (Wedding Feast):

The Walima is a celebratory feast hosted by the groom's family after the wedding. It is a way to announce and celebrate the union with family and friends. It is considered a Sunnah (tradition) of Prophet Muhammad to host a Walima after the marriage.
